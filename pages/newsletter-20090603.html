Hot off the press ... Artsmark Gold

It is with the greatest pleasure and pride that I can announce that the school has been awarded from the Arts Council the Artsmark Gold award. This represents the highest level of achievement a school can attain in Arts education and it recognises outstanding provision made in Art, Dance, Drama and Music across the school, from PTA art exhibitions to maypole dancing, taking part in school productions to learning songs in 'Big Sing'. It reflects the hard work by the whole school community in the wider arts.

Each application goes through a rigorous five-step assessment process which includes a visit to the school to ensure that the strict criteria for Artsmark have been met. It can be achieved at three levels: Artsmark, Artsmark Silver and Artsmark Gold so we are especially delighted with this judgement!

I should like to thank the staff, in particular, for their hard work and enthusiasm in the many arts opportunities they offer the children that has led to the school receiving this prestigious award.

Reminders

Sun cream

We have previously asked the children to bring in sun cream to be put on during the school day. There are now products that last 12 hours or more so we are requesting that sun cream be applied before your child comes to school and we would prefer no creams to be brought into school during the warm weather.

Sunhats

Children spend almost an hour outside each day; latest health advice encourages children to wear a sunhat. Please ensure hats are clearly marked with your child's name and preferably class. We have limited shady areas in school so sun protection is vital. Please try to leave a sunhat in your child's drawer so that they have one in school every day.
