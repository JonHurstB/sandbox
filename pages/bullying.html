Anti-Bullying Policy

What is Bullying

Bullying means that one person (or a group) is deliberately and knowingly hurting another, mentally and/or physically, for fun “just because they can”. This happens on repeated occasions and is not usually a “one-off” incident. The victim has their confidence so undermined that they allow this to continue and feel powerless to stop the torment.

Sadly, this happens in all walks of life – for adults as well as children.

What is not Bullying

  • A child “falling out” with another and having a disagreement.
  • A child bumping into another in the playground.
  • Playing boisterously or loudly
  • A “one-off” angry outburst.

Strategies Used

At Churchill Primary School we are very sensitive to real bullying. We have a policy and strategies in place to deal quickly and sensitively with both the bully and the victim.

As soon as we find out about incidents of bullying:

  1. The “Bully” and the “Victim” are listened to separately.
  2. All parties are then brought together and given the opportunity to share their views of the incident.
  3. The “Bully” and the “Victim” are set targets and report back the success of these.
  4. Incidents may be shared with the whole class and when necessary, discussion could take place in order to support class members.
  5. If these strategies are not successful, professional advice is sought. This could include assertiveness training or counseling.

At Churchill Primary we teach children skills to enable them to live happily together throughout their lives.
